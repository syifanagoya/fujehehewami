    Obituary of Mary Carol Majerus

    Life and Family


    Mary Carol Majerus, a beloved resident of Shakopee, passed away on December 31, 2023, at the age of 88. Born as Carol Wermerskirchen on May 17, 1935, in New Market, Minnesota, she was the daughter of Joseph and Laura Wermerskirchen. Carol grew up in a loving family and was known for her kind and compassionate nature. She cherished her family and had a deep bond with her siblings, Greg and Jim Wermerskirchen.

    Marriage and Children

    In the fourth grade, Carol met the love of her life, Richard “Butch” Majerus. Their connection grew stronger over the years, and they exchanged vows on August 13, 1955. Together, Carol and Butch embarked on a beautiful journey of love and companionship that lasted for 62 remarkable years. They were blessed with five daughters and one son, Barbara Farrell, Yvonne (Gene) Dodge, Larry (Lori) Majerus, Mary Hylbom, Liz (Chad) Cameron, and Cliff Miller. Carol and Butch were devoted parents who created countless cherished memories with their children.

    Professional Life and Hobbies


    Throughout her life, Carol pursued various professional endeavors. She worked in the bakery at Juba’s Grocery Store, where her warm smile and delicious treats brightened the days of many customers. Carol also had a successful career as a realtor and later as a bank teller before retiring. In addition to her professional life, she had a passion for quilting and was an active member of the Shakopee Quilt Club. For over 20 years, Carol led the St. Mark’s Quilting Mission, using her talent to create beautiful quilts for those in need.

    Carol’s life was filled with love, laughter, and a deep sense of purpose. She was a devoted wife, a loving mother, and a cherished friend. Her kind heart and gentle spirit touched the lives of everyone she encountered. Carol’s memory will forever live on in the hearts of her 24 grandchildren, 49 great-grandchildren, and five great-great-grandchildren. She will be dearly missed but never forgotten.
